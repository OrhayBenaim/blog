# PulseForm TikTok Strategy - Brainstorming Session

## Context
PulseForm is a fitness brand targeting 18-28 year olds. They sell resistance bands and home workout programs. Their voice is motivating but not preachy.

---

**Me:** I need a TikTok content strategy for "PulseForm" -- a fitness brand targeting 18-28 year olds. They sell resistance bands and home workout programs. Their voice is motivating but not preachy.

**Claude:** What's PulseForm's main goal with TikTok?

1. **Brand awareness** - Get the name out there, build a following, become recognizable in the fitness space
2. **Direct sales** - Drive traffic to product pages, convert viewers into buyers
3. **Community building** - Create a tribe of PulseForm users who share their workouts and progress
4. **Content-first approach** - Become a go-to fitness content account that happens to sell products

I'd lean toward option 4 since it tends to perform best on TikTok for brands targeting 18-28 year olds. Hard selling doesn't work well with that demographic on that platform.

**Me:** Brand awareness. We want to get the name out there first.

**Claude:** Got it, brand awareness is the priority. That makes sense for a newer brand trying to establish itself.

What kind of content style fits PulseForm's "motivating but not preachy" voice best?

1. **Quick workout clips** - 15-30 second resistance band exercises people can try immediately. High utility, easy to share.
2. **Transformation/progress content** - Before/after stories, "day 1 vs day 30" style content showing real results with their bands.
3. **Trend-riding fitness content** - Take trending TikTok sounds and formats, put a fitness spin on them. More entertainment, more reach.
4. **Creator-led personality content** - Build around 1-2 faces who become the brand. Think gym bro/sis energy but approachable.

My recommendation would be a mix of 1 and 3. Quick workout clips give people a reason to follow (value), and trend-riding gives the algorithm a reason to push the content (discoverability).

**Me:** Trend-riding fitness content. That's the play.

**Claude:** Trend-riding is smart for brand awareness. The algorithm rewards content that uses trending formats, and fitness is one of the easiest niches to adapt trends into.

How should PulseForm show up in the content without being salesy?

1. **Product as prop** - The resistance bands just happen to be in every video. No callouts, no "link in bio" pressure. People notice and ask about them naturally.
2. **Branded challenges** - Create a signature move or challenge that uses the bands. Something catchy enough that others recreate it.
3. **Subtle watermark/aesthetic** - Consistent color scheme, logo placement, and visual style that makes every video instantly recognizable as PulseForm even on mute.

I'd go with 1 as the foundation. On TikTok, the moment something feels like an ad, people scroll. If the bands are just there while someone does something entertaining, curiosity does the selling for you.

**Me:** The consistent visual aesthetic approach. Strong branding without the hard sell.

---

## Direction Summary
- **Goal:** Brand awareness
- **Content style:** Trend-riding fitness content (trending sounds + formats with a fitness twist)
- **Brand presence:** Consistent visual identity (color palette, logo placement, recognizable aesthetic). No hard selling.
