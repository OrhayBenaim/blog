# PulseForm TikTok Content Strategy

**Brand:** PulseForm - Resistance Bands & Home Workout Programs
**Target Audience:** 18-28 year olds (Gen Z core, younger Millennials)
**Primary Goal:** Brand awareness - build a recognizable presence and loyal following
**Brand Voice:** Motivating but not preachy. Think "gym best friend," not "drill sergeant."

---

## 1. Content Pillars and Themes

PulseForm's TikTok content should follow a **40/30/20/10 split** across four pillars. This ratio ensures the feed feels valuable and entertaining rather than like a product catalog.

### Pillar 1: Education (40% of content)

**Theme: "Band Science"**
Quick, digestible fitness tips that position PulseForm as the authority on resistance band training. These videos answer the questions your audience is already searching for.

- Proper form breakdowns for resistance band exercises
- "Most people do this wrong" correction videos
- Muscle activation comparisons (bands vs. free weights)
- Progressive overload techniques with bands
- Recovery and stretching routines using bands
- Myth-busting content about home workouts

**Why 40%:** Educational content gets saved and shared at the highest rates on TikTok. Saves signal the algorithm that your content has lasting value, which pushes it to broader audiences over days and weeks rather than just hours.

### Pillar 2: Entertainment (30% of content)

**Theme: "Band Life"**
Pure entertainment that happens to feature resistance bands and fitness culture. This is where trend-riding lives. The goal is to make people laugh, relate, or feel something -- the brand association comes naturally.

- Trending sound remixes with a fitness twist
- Relatable gym humor and home workout struggles
- "Day in the life" content featuring PulseForm products organically
- Transformation reveals set to trending audio
- Duets and stitches reacting to fitness content
- Challenge participation and creation

**Why 30%:** Entertainment drives follows. People follow accounts that make them feel good. This pillar builds the community that the educational pillar retains.

### Pillar 3: Inspiration (20% of content)

**Theme: "Pulse Check"**
Motivational content that connects emotionally. Real stories, real progress, real people using PulseForm products in their actual lives. No stock footage energy.

- User transformation stories (before/after with narrative)
- "Why I started" personal journey content
- Community spotlights featuring real PulseForm users
- Mental health and fitness connection content
- "Small wins" celebration videos
- Morning routine and habit-building content

**Why 20%:** Inspirational content drives comments and emotional engagement. Comments are the highest-weighted engagement signal in TikTok's algorithm after watch time.

### Pillar 4: Promotional (10% of content)

**Theme: "The Drop"**
Subtle product showcases that feel native to TikTok. No hard selling. No "link in bio" pressure. The product should feel like a natural part of the content, not the reason for it.

- New product reveals using trending transition formats
- "What's in my gym bag" featuring PulseForm products
- Product comparison content (PulseForm bands vs. generic)
- Behind-the-scenes of product development
- Limited edition or seasonal product teasers
- Aesthetic product shots set to trending audio

**Why only 10%:** The fastest way to kill a TikTok account is to make it feel like an ad channel. At 10%, promotional content feels like an occasional treat rather than a sales pitch. The other 90% does the real selling by building trust and affinity.

## 2. Specific Content Ideas with Hooks

Below are 24 ready-to-produce video concepts organized by pillar. Each includes the hook line (what viewers see/hear in the first 3 seconds), a full description of the video, and production notes.

**Video 1: "The exercise everyone does wrong with bands"**
- **Hook (0-3s):** Text on screen: "Stop doing bicep curls like this." Creator holds a resistance band with visibly incorrect form.

- **Audio:** Trending explanatory sound or original voiceover.
- **Duration:** 15-30 seconds.
- **CTA:** Text overlay at end: "Save this so you don't forget." (Drives saves.)

**Video 2: "3 band exercises that replace an entire gym"**

- **Duration:** 30-45 seconds.
- **CTA:** "Which one are you trying first? Comment below."

- **Hook (0-3s):** Slow-motion footage of a resistance band snapping mid-exercise (staged safely). Text: "This keeps happening to you."
- **Description:** Walk through three common causes: storing bands in direct sunlight, overstretching past recommended length, using bands with visible wear. Show PulseForm bands and their durability features without making it a hard sell.

- **CTA:** "Duet this with your band horror story."

## 3. Posting Schedule and Frequency

### Weekly Posting Cadence

**Target: 5-7 posts per week.** TikTok rewards consistency and volume more than any other platform. The algorithm needs data points to understand your audience, and more posts means more opportunities for one to break through.

| Day | Pillar | Content Type | Best Posting Time (EST) |
|-----|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| Monday | Education | Form tip or exercise breakdown | 7:00 AM or 12:00 PM |
| Tuesday | Entertainment | Trend remix or comedy skit | 6:00 PM or 9:00 PM |
| Wednesday | Education | Myth-busting or comparison | 7:00 AM or 12:00 PM |
| Thursday | Inspiration | Transformation or personal story | 6:00 PM or 9:00 PM |
| Friday | Entertainment | Trend-riding content | 12:00 PM or 7:00 PM |
| Saturday | Education or Inspiration | Flexible based on trending content | 10:00 AM or 2:00 PM |
| Sunday | Promotional (biweekly) | Product showcase or BTS | 10:00 AM or 2:00 PM |

### Posting Time Strategy

- **Morning posts (7:00 AM):** Catch the pre-workout and commute audience. Best for educational content people save for later.
- **Midday posts (12:00 PM):** Lunch break scrollers. Quick, punchy entertainment and tips perform best.
- **Evening posts (6:00-9:00 PM):** Peak TikTok usage. Best for entertainment, trends, and longer inspirational content.
- **Weekend posts (10:00 AM - 2:00 PM):** Relaxed browsing. Good for longer content and promotional pieces.

### Important Scheduling Notes

- These times are starting points. After 30 days of posting, check TikTok Analytics for when your specific followers are most active and adjust accordingly.
- Never sacrifice content quality for schedule adherence. If you don't have a strong video for a scheduled slot, skip it. A mediocre video hurts more than a missed day.
- Leave 1-2 "flex slots" per week open for reactive trend content that needs to go out immediately (see Section 7 on trend-riding).

### Content Batching Approach

- **Batch filming:** Dedicate two half-days per week to filming. Monday morning for the week's educational content. Thursday morning for entertainment and inspiration.
- **Trend content:** Cannot be batched. These must be produced and posted within 24-48 hours of a trend emerging. Keep equipment ready for quick shoots.
- **Editing pipeline:** Aim to have 3-5 videos edited and ready to post at all times. This buffer prevents gaps during busy weeks.

## 4. Visual Identity Guidelines for TikTok

### Color Palette

Every PulseForm video should be instantly recognizable in a scroll. This means consistent visual elements without making the content feel overproduced or corporate.

**Primary Colors:**
- **PulseForm Orange (#FF6B35):** The signature brand color. Used in text overlays, borders, and accent elements.
- **Deep Black (#1A1A1A):** Background for text, used in contrast with the orange.
- **Clean White (#FAFAFA):** Text color on dark backgrounds, clean space.

**Secondary Colors:**
- **Warm Gray (#E8E4E1):** Subtle backgrounds, secondary text.
- **Electric Blue (#4DA8DA):** Accent for educational content callouts and data points.

### Typography

- **Primary Font for Text Overlays:** Bold, sans-serif (use TikTok's built-in bold text or a consistent custom font like Montserrat Bold).
- **All text overlays use sentence case**, never all caps (all caps reads as yelling and feels aggressive).
- **Text size:** Large enough to read on mobile without squinting. Test on an actual phone screen before posting.
- **Text placement:** Always in the center-upper third of the frame. Avoid the bottom 20% (covered by TikTok UI) and the right side (covered by engagement buttons).

### Logo Placement

- **Subtle watermark:** Small PulseForm logo in the upper-left corner of every video. Semi-transparent (60-70% opacity). Present but never distracting.
- **End card:** Final 2-3 seconds of every video should include a clean PulseForm logo animation or static logo. Keep it brief -- just enough for brand recall.
- **Product visibility:** When PulseForm bands appear in videos, ensure the logo/branding on the bands themselves is visible but not artificially centered. Natural, not staged.

### Filming Standards

- **Lighting:** Consistent warm lighting. Natural light preferred. If using ring lights, avoid the harsh "influencer spotlight" look. Aim for soft, even lighting that looks like a well-lit room, not a studio.
- **Background:** Clean but lived-in. Real homes, real apartments, real spaces. Not sterile white backgrounds. The audience should feel like they could be in the same room. A consistent filming location helps with brand recognition.
- **Camera angle:** Eye-level or slightly above for talking-head content. Full-body straight-on for exercise demonstrations. Avoid low angles (unflattering) and extreme high angles (hard to see exercises).
- **Resolution:** Always film in 1080x1920 (9:16 vertical). Never upload horizontal content cropped to vertical.

### Brand Element Consistency

- **Resistance bands visible in 80%+ of videos**, even if they are in the background. This creates subconscious product association.
- **Consistent intro pattern:** Not a formal intro (those kill retention). Instead, a recognizable visual cue -- like the creator always holding a band in the first frame, or the PulseForm orange color appearing in the first second.
- **Consistent creator appearance:** If using a single creator/face, maintain consistent styling. The person becomes the brand's visual anchor on TikTok.

## 6. Growth Tactics Specific to TikTok's Algorithm

### Understanding the Algorithm (As of Early 2026)

TikTok's recommendation algorithm weights these signals, roughly in this order:

1. **Watch time / completion rate** -- The single most important metric. Did people watch the whole video? Did they rewatch it?
2. **Engagement velocity** -- How quickly do likes, comments, shares, and saves accumulate after posting?
3. **Shares** -- Shares to messages and other platforms signal exceptional content.
4. **Saves** -- Saves signal utility and lasting value.
5. **Comments** -- Both volume and the engagement within comments (replies, likes on comments).
6. **Profile visits and follows from the video** -- Signals that the content made people want more.
7. **Content category signals** -- Hashtags, captions, on-screen text, and audio help TikTok categorize and distribute to relevant audiences.

### Tactic 1: Optimize for Completion Rate Above All Else

- **Keep videos as short as they need to be.** A 15-second video watched fully outperforms a 60-second video watched halfway. If you can make the point in 15 seconds, do not stretch to 30.
- **Front-load the hook.** The first 3 seconds determine whether someone swipes. Every video must open with a visual or textual hook that creates curiosity, tension, or surprise.
- **Use open loops.** Start with a question or promise ("The exercise everyone does wrong") that can only be resolved by watching to the end.
- **End with intention.** The last 2 seconds should either prompt a rewatch (surprising ending) or drive engagement (question, CTA). Avoid fade-outs or trailing endings.

### Tactic 2: Engineer Engagement Velocity

The first 30-60 minutes after posting are critical. TikTok tests your video with a small audience first. If that initial batch engages strongly, the video gets pushed to progressively larger audiences.

- **Post when your audience is active** (see posting schedule in Section 3).
- **Respond to every comment in the first hour.** Each response counts as additional engagement and shows the algorithm the comment section is active.
- **Ask polarizing but friendly questions.** "Bands vs. weights -- which side are you on?" generates more comments than "Like if you agree."
- **Pin a provocative or funny comment** to encourage more people to reply.
- **Use the "reply to comment with a video" feature** to generate follow-up content that links back to the original.

### Tactic 3: Maximize Shares and Saves

Shares and saves are worth more than likes because they require more intentional action.

- **For shares:** Create content people want to send to friends. Relatable humor ("tag your gym buddy"), useful tips ("send this to someone who needs it"), and challenge content all drive shares.
- **For saves:** Educational content with lasting value drives saves. Workout routines, form guides, and exercise lists are inherently saveable. Use text overlays like "Save this for your next workout" explicitly.

### Tactic 4: Cross-Pollinate Content

- **Stitch and Duet aggressively.** React to popular fitness creators' content. This puts your face next to established creators and exposes you to their audiences.
- **Comment on larger creators' videos** with genuinely useful or funny comments. Top comments on viral videos can drive thousands of profile visits.
- **Collaborate with creators** in the 10K-100K follower range. They are accessible, affordable, and their audiences are highly engaged.

### Tactic 5: Leverage TikTok Series and Playlists

- **Create content series** with numbered episodes: "Band Exercise of the Day #1," "Band Exercise of the Day #2," etc. Series encourage profile visits to watch previous episodes and follow for future ones.
- **Organize videos into playlists** by topic: "Full Body Band Workouts," "Band Tips," "PulseForm Community." This increases watch time per profile visit.

### Tactic 6: Post Consistently Through the Dip

Most TikTok accounts quit during the "dip" -- the period after initial excitement where views plateau or drop. This typically happens around weeks 3-6. The algorithm is still learning your content and audience. Push through with consistent posting and the algorithm will find your audience.

- **Minimum viable consistency:** 5 posts per week for 90 consecutive days before evaluating strategy changes.
- **Do not delete underperforming videos.** They contribute to the algorithm's understanding of your content. Some videos go viral days or weeks after posting.

## 7. How to Ride Trends Effectively

### The Trend-Riding Process

Trend-riding is PulseForm's primary entertainment strategy. The goal is to take whatever is trending on TikTok and give it a resistance band or fitness twist. This requires speed, creativity, and a clear process.

### Step 1: Trend Monitoring (Daily - 20 minutes)

Every morning, one team member spends 20 minutes on the following:

- **Scroll the For You Page** for 10 minutes on a "clean" TikTok account (not the brand account). Note any sounds, formats, or effects appearing 3+ times.
- **Check the TikTok Creative Center** (creativeCenter.tiktok.com) for trending sounds, hashtags, and creators.
- **Check TikTok's search/discover page** for trending topics.
- **Monitor competitor accounts** (5-6 fitness brands) for trends they are jumping on.
- **Check cross-platform:** Instagram Reels trending audio often crosses to TikTok within 48 hours and vice versa.

### Step 2: Trend Evaluation (5 minutes per trend)

Not every trend is worth riding. Evaluate each potential trend with these three questions:

1. **Can we connect this to resistance bands or fitness naturally?** If the connection feels forced, skip it. Audiences can smell inauthenticity instantly.
2. **Is this trend still rising or already peaking?** Check the Creative Center for trend trajectory. Ideally, jump on trends in their first 48-72 hours. If a trend has been around for more than a week, it is likely too late.
3. **Does this trend align with our brand voice?** PulseForm is motivating and fun, not edgy, political, or mean-spirited. If a trend relies on negativity, mockery of others, or controversy, skip it.

### Step 3: Concept Development (15 minutes)

Once a trend passes evaluation:

- **Watch the top 10 videos** using the trending sound or format.
- **Identify the core pattern.** What makes this trend work? Is it the surprise? The relatability? The visual transition?
- **Write a one-sentence PulseForm adaptation.** Example: Trending format is "things that just make sense." PulseForm version: "Things that just make sense: doing your entire workout without leaving your living room."
- **Plan the shot.** What do we need? Creator, bands, specific location, any props? Keep production minimal -- overproduced trend content underperforms.

### Step 4: Production (30-60 minutes)

- **Film the video.** Keep it simple. Trend content should feel spontaneous even if it is planned.
- **Edit quickly.** Use TikTok's native editor when possible -- natively edited videos reportedly receive a small algorithm boost.
- **Add PulseForm brand elements** (subtle logo, brand colors in text overlays) without disrupting the trend format.

### Step 5: Post and Monitor (immediate + 2 hours)

- **Post immediately.** Trend content has a short shelf life. Do not wait for the "optimal" posting time if it means the trend will age.
- **Monitor engagement** for the first 2 hours. If it is gaining traction, engage heavily in comments to boost velocity.
- **Assess performance** after 48 hours. Document what worked and why for future trend-riding decisions.

### Trend Categories and Examples

**Sound Trends:**
A trending audio clip that thousands of creators use with their own visuals.
- Example: If a dramatic "reveal" sound is trending, use it for a before/after physique reveal or a "what my workout looks like vs. what it feels like" comparison.

**Format Trends:**
A specific video structure (like "put a finger down" or "tell me without telling me") that can be adapted.
- Example: "Tell me you work out at home without telling me you work out at home" -- show the door anchor marks, the band storage drawer, the yoga mat indent in the carpet.

**Effect Trends:**
A specific TikTok filter or visual effect that is going viral.
- Example: If a body-scanning or x-ray effect is trending, use it to "show" which muscles a specific band exercise activates.

**Challenge Trends:**
A specific physical or creative challenge that spreads through participation.
- Example: If a strength challenge is trending, adapt it to show resistance band versions and tag other fitness creators to participate.

These indicate whether the awareness is meaningful -- are people actually connecting with the brand?

### Content Performance Metrics

These help optimize the strategy over time.

| Metric | What It Tells You | Action If Low |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| **Hook Rate** (% who watch past 3 seconds) | Whether your hooks are working | Test different hook styles, make first frame more visually arresting |
| **Completion Rate** | Whether content holds attention | Shorten videos, improve pacing, stronger mid-video hooks |
| **Share-to-Like Ratio** | Whether content is share-worthy | Create more "tag a friend" and relatable content |
| **Save-to-Like Ratio** | Whether content has lasting value | Create more educational/reference content |
| **Follower Conversion Rate** (follows / profile views) | Whether your profile converts visitors | Optimize bio, pin best videos, improve profile aesthetic |
| **Viral Hit Rate** (videos exceeding 10x average views) | Whether you are creating breakout content | Analyze viral videos for patterns, double down on what works |

### Reporting Cadence

- **Daily:** Quick check on previous day's video performance (views, engagement rate, any comments needing response).
- **Weekly:** Review all videos from the past 7 days. Identify top and bottom performers. Note any trends in what worked.
- **Monthly:** Full analytics review. Compare against targets. Adjust content pillar ratios, posting times, and hashtag strategy based on data. Document learnings.
- **Quarterly:** Strategic review. Are we hitting brand awareness goals? Should we adjust the overall approach? Plan the next quarter's branded challenge and creator partnerships.

### Vanity Metrics to Ignore

- **Follower count in isolation.** 5K engaged followers are worth more than 50K passive ones. Always evaluate follower count alongside engagement rate.
- **Individual video view count.** One viral video does not make a strategy. Look at average views across all content over 30 days.
- **Likes alone.** Likes are the lowest-effort engagement. Saves, shares, and comments are far more meaningful signals.

- [ ] Video concept aligns with one of the four content pillars

### Production
- [ ] Filmed in 9:16 vertical format at 1080x1920 minimum
- [ ] Lighting is warm and consistent
- [ ] Background is clean but authentic (real space, not sterile)
- [ ] Audio is clear (no background noise for voiceover content)
- [ ] PulseForm bands/products visible in frame where relevant

### Post-Production
- [ ] Text overlays use brand fonts and colors
- [ ] Text placed in safe zones (not bottom 20%, not right 15%)
- [ ] PulseForm watermark/logo present (subtle, upper-left)
- [ ] Video length is as short as possible while delivering the full message
- [ ] Trending sound properly synced if applicable
- [ ] Thumbnail/cover frame is compelling

### Publishing
- [ ] Caption written (short, conversational, includes CTA)
- [ ] Hashtags added (5-8, proper mix)
- [ ] Posted at optimal time for the content type
- [ ] Team member assigned to monitor comments for first 2 hours
- [ ] Video saved to content archive for cross-platform repurposing
