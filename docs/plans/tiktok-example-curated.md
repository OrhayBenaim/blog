# Curated Material for Blog Post

## Selected Brainstorming Exchanges

These 3 exchanges show how the brainstorming narrowed from a vague idea to a clear direction.

### Exchange 1: Defining the Goal

> **Me:** I need a TikTok content strategy for "PulseForm" -- a fitness brand targeting 18-28 year olds. They sell resistance bands and home workout programs. Their voice is motivating but not preachy.

> **Claude:** What's PulseForm's main goal with TikTok?
>
> 1. **Brand awareness** - Get the name out there, build a following
> 2. **Direct sales** - Drive traffic to product pages
> 3. **Community building** - Create a tribe of PulseForm users
> 4. **Content-first approach** - Become a go-to fitness account that happens to sell products
>
> I'd lean toward option 4 since it tends to perform best on TikTok for brands targeting 18-28 year olds. Hard selling doesn't work well with that demographic on that platform.

> **Me:** Brand awareness. We want to get the name out there first.

### Exchange 2: Finding the Content Style

> **Claude:** What kind of content style fits PulseForm's "motivating but not preachy" voice best?
>
> 1. **Quick workout clips** - 15-30 second resistance band exercises. High utility, easy to share.
> 2. **Transformation/progress content** - Before/after stories, "day 1 vs day 30" style.
> 3. **Trend-riding fitness content** - Take trending TikTok sounds and formats, put a fitness spin on them. More entertainment, more reach.
> 4. **Creator-led personality content** - Build around 1-2 faces who become the brand.
>
> My recommendation would be a mix of 1 and 3. Quick workout clips give people a reason to follow (value), and trend-riding gives the algorithm a reason to push the content (discoverability).

> **Me:** Trend-riding fitness content. That's the play.

### Exchange 3: Brand Presence Without Selling

> **Claude:** How should PulseForm show up in the content without being salesy?
>
> 1. **Product as prop** - The resistance bands just happen to be in every video. No callouts, no "link in bio" pressure.
> 2. **Branded challenges** - Create a signature move or challenge that uses the bands.
> 3. **Subtle watermark/aesthetic** - Consistent color scheme, logo placement, and visual style that makes every video instantly recognizable as PulseForm even on mute.
>
> I'd go with 1 as the foundation. On TikTok, the moment something feels like an ad, people scroll.

> **Me:** The consistent visual aesthetic approach. Strong branding without the hard sell.

## Best Parts of TikTok Strategy Output

### Content Pillars (40/30/20/10 Split)

The strategy organized content into four pillars with a specific ratio:

- **Education (40%)** - "Band Science": form tips, myth-busting, exercise breakdowns. Educational content gets saved and shared at the highest rates on TikTok.
- **Entertainment (30%)** - "Band Life": trend remixes, gym humor, relatable home workout struggles. Entertainment drives follows.
- **Inspiration (20%)** - "Pulse Check": real transformation stories, personal journeys, community spotlights. Inspirational content drives comments, the highest-weighted engagement signal after watch time.
- **Promotional (10%)** - "The Drop": subtle product showcases that feel native to TikTok. No hard selling. The other 90% does the real selling by building trust.

### Standout Content Ideas

**"POV: You try to work out at home but..."**
- Hook: "POV: home workout attempt #47"
- Comedy skit where creator keeps getting interrupted -- roommate walks through, cat attacks the band, door anchor slips, phone falls. Final shot: one perfect rep, triumphant face.

**"She said bands don't build real muscle"**
- Hook: Text message screenshot: "Lol resistance bands? That's not a real workout."
- Creator doesn't argue. Just shows their workout -- intense, sweating, shaking during last reps. No words needed. Let the visuals speak. End with a calm look to camera and a subtle flex.

**"Resistance band ASMR but make it chaotic"**
- Start with satisfying ASMR content (bands stretching, smooth movements) then gradually escalate into chaos (tangling, weird noises, absurd "exercises"). Subvert the ASMR trend.

### Posting Schedule Highlights

- 5-7 posts per week
- Each day assigned a specific pillar (Mon/Wed/Sat = Education, Tue/Fri = Entertainment, Thu = Inspiration, biweekly Sunday = Promotional)
- 1-2 "flex slots" per week kept open for reactive trend content
- Batch filming: two half-days per week, but trend content produced within 24-48 hours

### Trend-Riding Process (Daily Workflow)

A clear 5-step process the strategy laid out:
1. **Monitor** (20 min/morning) - Scroll For You Page, check TikTok Creative Center, monitor competitors
2. **Evaluate** (5 min/trend) - Can we connect this to fitness naturally? Is it still rising or peaking? Does it match our voice?
3. **Concept** (15 min) - Watch top 10 videos using the trend, identify the core pattern, write a one-sentence PulseForm adaptation
4. **Produce** (30-60 min) - Film, edit in TikTok's native editor, add brand elements
5. **Post and Monitor** (immediate + 2 hours) - Post immediately, engage in comments

### Visual Identity

- **PulseForm Orange (#FF6B35)** as signature brand color
- Subtle watermark in upper-left corner (60-70% opacity)
- Resistance bands visible in 80%+ of videos
- Consistent warm lighting, real spaces (not sterile studios)

### Growth Metrics

Targets for months 1-3: 500K views/month, 5K followers by month 3, 8%+ engagement rate, 70%+ average completion rate.
